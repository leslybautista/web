Understanding Political Systems

Democracy

Democracy is broadly understood to mean 'rule by the people'. In practice, it is often defined as people choosing their leaders in free and fair elections. Democracy gives citizens the right to influence important decisions over their own lives and allows them to hold their leaders accountable.

Autocratization

Autocratization is the process of moving away from democracy towards autocracy. It can be understood as a gradual erosion of democratic institutions and practices, leading to a more authoritarian regime. This includes restrictions on political freedoms and media censorship.

Democratic Trends in the Americas

El Salvador's Authoritarian Turn

Journal of Democracy

President Nayib Bukele has used his legislative supermajority to dismiss top judicial figures, deepening the democratic crisis in El Salvador and raising concerns about institutional independence.

Honduras Reclaims Democracy

Progressive International

After years of political violence, Hondurans elected Xiomara Castro in 2021. This peaceful transition marks a significant democratic breakthrough following the 2009 coup.

Dominican Republic's Progress

International IDEA

Under President Luis Abinader, the Dominican Republic has seen improvements in rule of law and civil accountability, positioning the country as a democratic bright spot in Latin America.
